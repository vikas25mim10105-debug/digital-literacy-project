# UPI Fraud Case Study

---

## Introduction
UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is one of the most common types of cybercrime today. Students and regular users are especially vulnerable because they may not be fully aware of online payment security practices.

---
## How the Fraud Happens

When the fraud happens it usually goes like this:

- The fraudster will send a message asking for money to be transferred or they will send a link through SMS, WhatsApp or Email.

The fraudster is trying to get money from the user. They want the user to verify their account.

- If the user clicks on the link or says it is okay then money will be taken out of the users account.

- The fraudster might even say they work at the bank or they might say they work in customer care. The user trusts them and gives them their information, which is what the fraudster wants the users details so they can do the fraud.

---
## Real-Life Example

Something bad happened to a student who was selling something online. A buyer sent them a message saying they wanted to pay using UPI.

- The buyer did not actually send any money they just sent a request for payment

- The student was then asked to put in their UPI PIN

- When they did that the money, from the students account went to the persons account

---

## Who is Targeted?

- People who do not really understand how UPI works

- People who trust messages or links from people they do not know

---

## Consequences

- The person loses money

- They feel very stressed and worried

- They also lose their information

- And they might even have their identity stolen

---

## This story shows us that we have to be very careful when we are making payments. If we are careful and pay attention we can avoid problems. Taking some steps to be safe can really help us avoid losing a lot of money. UPI payments can be safe if we use UPI correctly and do not trust everyone.
